French A1 Literature
Books and Comics

L'élève Ducobu by Zidrou & Godi, 1997

A classic hilarious French-Dutch tale of Ducobu, a lazy and creative student who tries to cheat his way through school with his shenanigans while outsmarting his strict teacher, Monsieur Latouche

Characteristics: Simple school-related vocab, Classroom dialogue, and easier to read with comic format

Skills Improved: Reading, Cultural Awareness



Le Petit Nicolas by René Goscinny & Jean-Jacques Sempé, 1959

A humorous and heartwarming tale about Nicolas, a mischievous French schoolboy, and his friends as they navigate childhood adventures and innocent chaos in 1950s France. A great intro to Goscinny's works, a famed author with other loved works, such as Astérix et Obelix.

Characteristics: Simple language, Childhood Expressions, Past-Tense Usage

Skills Improved: Reading, Cultural Awareness



Kiffe Kiffe Demain by Faïza Guène, 2004

A witty and raw coming-of-age story of Doria, a Moroccan-French teenager living in the suburbs of Paris, facing social struggles and dreaming of a brighter future. Explore Francophone culture and global issues, in a simple format!

Characteristics: Everyday slang, Urban expressions, Informal Storytelling

Skills Improved: Listening, Reading, Speaking, Cultural Awareness
